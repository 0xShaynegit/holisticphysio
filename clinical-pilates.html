What clinical Pilates focuses on

  • Core strength and stability
  • Postural alignment
  • Controlled, mindful movement
  • Rehabilitation after injury or surgery
  • Prevention of future injuries

What is clinical Pilates?

A form of therapeutic exercise based on the original Pilates method, adapted by physiotherapists using evidence-based principles. Every session begins with a thorough assessment, so your program is tailored to your specific goals, whether that's easing lower back pain or enhancing sports performance.

  1. We find your core

    We show you exactly where your core muscles are and whether you're activating them, not just guessing.

  2. Mat and equipment-based work

    Sessions include mat Pilates as well as the Oov, Pilates ring and exercise ball.

  3. A home program

    Simple, personalised exercises to continue between sessions.

Good questions

Never done Pilates before? These are the questions we hear most.

I've never done Pilates, will I keep up?

Every session is one-on-one and paced entirely to you. There's no class to keep up with, we start at your level and build from there.

Will it hurt if I'm already injured?

Sessions are supervised by a physiotherapist and adapted around your injury, so movement stays safe and within your comfortable range.

Do I need to be fit already?

No. Clinical Pilates is designed to build strength and confidence from wherever you're starting, not for people who are already fit.

What should I wear?

Comfortable, stretchy clothing you can move freely in, and socks or bare feet.

Do I need a doctor's referral?

No, you can book directly. A referral is only required for WorkCover, Veterans' Affairs, CTP or a Medicare Chronic Disease Management plan.

Can I claim on my private health fund?

In most cases, yes. The clinic is registered with all major health funds for physiotherapy, acupuncture and Chinese herbal medicine, so most policies allow you to claim on the spot through HICAPS and just pay the gap. Check with your fund to confirm your level of cover.
