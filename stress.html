How stress shows up in the body

  • Tight shoulders, jaw clenching and back pain
  • Poor sleep and racing thoughts at night
  • Fatigue and low energy through the day
  • Digestive upset and low immunity

The stress response

When your brain detects a challenge, it triggers the fight-or-flight response: heart rate rises, muscles tighten, breathing shortens and digestion slows. This works well in emergencies, but modern stressors like deadlines and financial pressure don't go away quickly, so the response stays switched on.

Good questions

Talking about stress isn't always easy. Here's what most people want to know first.

Is it normal to feel nervous about discussing stress?

Very. This is a private, judgment-free space, and you only need to share what feels comfortable.

Will treatment interact with my anxiety medication?

We take a full history including any medications, and treatment is designed to work safely alongside your existing care.

How quickly can I expect to feel calmer?

Many people feel noticeably calmer after their first session, with more lasting change building over a few weeks of treatment.

Do I need a doctor's referral?

No, you can book directly. A referral is only required for WorkCover, Veterans' Affairs, CTP or a Medicare Chronic Disease Management plan.

Can I claim on my private health fund?

In most cases, yes. The clinic is registered with all major health funds for physiotherapy, acupuncture and Chinese herbal medicine, so most policies allow you to claim on the spot through HICAPS and just pay the gap. Check with your fund to confirm your level of cover.
