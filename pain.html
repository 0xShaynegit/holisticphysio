Pain doesn't only affect your body, it impacts your mood, energy, sleep and daily life. Instead of just masking the symptoms, we aim to find the root cause and treat it with a holistic mix of therapies.

What influences pain

  • Tissue damage or injury: sprains, strains, fractures
  • Inflammation: helpful for healing, harmful in excess
  • Nerve sensitisation, common in chronic conditions
  • Posture and biomechanics, from long hours sitting
  • Muscle tension, often worsened by stress
  • Hormonal changes and lifestyle factors

Acute vs chronic pain

Acute pain is short-term, usually from an injury or sudden illness: your body's alarm system. Chronic pain lasts longer than three months, even after the original injury has healed, because the nervous system has become hypersensitive. Modern neuroscience calls this neuroplasticity: the longer pain continues, the more the nervous system reinforces those pathways.

Will treatment make my pain worse before it gets better?

We work within your comfort level throughout. Some mild soreness after hands-on treatment is normal, but we won't push you into unnecessary pain.

I've tried other treatments that didn't work, will this be different?

Combining physiotherapy, acupuncture and Chinese herbal medicine lets us address the cause from multiple angles, not just repeat what hasn't worked.

How long until I feel some relief?

Many people notice a change within the first few sessions, though chronic pain often needs a longer, layered approach. We'll be upfront about what to expect.

Do I need a doctor's referral?

No, you can book directly. A referral is only required for WorkCover, Veterans' Affairs, CTP or a Medicare Chronic Disease Management plan.

Can I claim on my private health fund?

In most cases, yes. The clinic is registered with all major health funds for physiotherapy, acupuncture and Chinese herbal medicine, so most policies allow you to claim on the spot through HICAPS and just pay the gap. Check with your fund to confirm your level of cover.
