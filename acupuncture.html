What is Acupuncture?

Acupuncture is a key component of Traditional Chinese Medicine, using very fine, sterile needles inserted into specific points on the body. These points regulate the nervous system, improve circulation and restore the smooth flow of energy, or Qi. Modern research shows acupuncture can also regulate hormones, reduce inflammation and activate the body's natural healing processes.

  1. Rooted in Traditional Chinese Medicine

    Thousands of years of clinical use, now supported by modern research into pain and inflammation.

  2. Ultra-fine, sterile needles

    Gently inserted into carefully chosen points, usually felt as warmth, heaviness or deep relaxation.

  3. Combined with our other therapies

    Often paired with physiotherapy, Chinese herbal medicine and clinical Pilates to address the root cause, not just the symptoms.

Good questions

A lot of people feel nervous before their first acupuncture session. Here's what usually helps.

Does acupuncture hurt?

The needles are ultra-fine, much thinner than an injection needle. Most people feel little more than a light tap, and many find the sessions deeply relaxing.

I'm scared of needles, can I still try it?

Yes, plenty of our patients started out nervous. We go at your pace, explain each step, and can start with just a few points to help you settle in.

Is acupuncture safe?

Yes. We use sterile, single-use needles and Sandra is accredited by the Australian Acupuncture and Chinese Medicine Association.

How many sessions before I notice a difference?

Many people feel a shift after their first or second session, though it varies by condition. We'll talk through what to expect for you specifically.

Do I need a doctor's referral?

No, you can book directly. A referral is only required for WorkCover, Veterans' Affairs, CTP or a Medicare Chronic Disease Management plan.

Can I claim on my private health fund?

In most cases, yes. The clinic is registered with all major health funds for physiotherapy, acupuncture and Chinese herbal medicine, so most policies allow you to claim on the spot through HICAPS and just pay the gap. Check with your fund to confirm your level of cover.
