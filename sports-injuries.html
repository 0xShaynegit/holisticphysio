Assessment, then a plan

Every injury is assessed to understand not just what's damaged, but why: biomechanics, load, strength imbalances and technique all play a part. Treatment addresses the injury and the contributing factors, so return to sport is safer and more lasting.

  1. Physiotherapy

    Manual therapy, targeted exercise and hands-on treatment for the injury itself.

  2. Clinical Pilates

    Rebuilding strength, control and stability through supervised, graded exercise.

  3. Acupuncture

    Pain relief and improved circulation to support recovery.

Good questions

Injuries can be as stressful mentally as they are physically. Here's what to expect.

Will I have to stop playing sport completely?

Rarely a full stop. Most plans keep you moving in some capacity while protecting the injury, with a graded return to full training.

Is it too late if I've had this injury for a while?

No, it's never too late to start treating an injury properly, even long-standing ones respond well to the right plan.

How long until I'm back training?

It depends on the injury, but we'll give you a realistic timeline after assessment, along with what you can safely do in the meantime.

Do I need a doctor's referral?

No, you can book directly. A referral is only required for WorkCover, Veterans' Affairs, CTP or a Medicare Chronic Disease Management plan.

Can I claim on my private health fund?

In most cases, yes. The clinic is registered with all major health funds for physiotherapy, acupuncture and Chinese herbal medicine, so most policies allow you to claim on the spot through HICAPS and just pay the gap. Check with your fund to confirm your level of cover.
